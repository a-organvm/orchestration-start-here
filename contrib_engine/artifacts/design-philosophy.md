# Systematic Cartography

A design philosophy where contribution networks are rendered as topographic maps of knowledge flow.

## Philosophy

In this movement, software ecosystems are terrain. Each repository is a settlement — its star count the population, its commit frequency the pulse, its open issues the unresolved tensions at the borders. The contributor who moves between settlements is not a visitor but a cartographer, tracing the invisible trade routes that connect one system of knowledge to another. The visual language is that of survey maps overlaid with network diagrams: precise, measured, yet revealing landscapes that no single inhabitant can see from ground level.

Color operates as altitude. Deep indigo marks the foundation — the internal system, the home territory. As knowledge flows outward toward external targets, hue shifts through violet into warm spectrum: rose for active engagement, gold for harvested returns. The gradient is not decorative but informational — a reader should intuit the direction of flow without needing labels. Every chromatic transition has been calibrated with the painstaking attention of a master cartographer ensuring contour lines meet at exact intervals.

Geometry serves as the skeletal system. Circles represent repositories, their radii proportional to influence. Lines between them carry weight — thickness encoding relationship strength, dash patterns distinguishing outbound contributions from inbound signals. The composition breathes with the precision of a geodetic survey: nothing placed approximately, every element positioned through calculation that has been labored over with the care of someone at the absolute top of their field. Nodes sit on invisible grid intersections. Connections follow minimal-crossing paths. The overall topology reveals structure that text alone cannot communicate.

Typography exists only as coordinate markers and settlement names — small, precise, monospaced characters that annotate without explaining. Numbers appear as data points: star counts, PR numbers, relationship scores. These are the marginal notes of the cartographer, not the narrative of the explorer. The work must appear meticulously crafted, the product of deep expertise and countless hours of refinement, as though each label was hand-set by a typographer who understood that even the smallest annotation participates in the visual harmony of the whole.

The overall impression should be of an artifact from an imaginary discipline — systematic contribution cartography — where the mapping of knowledge exchange between software ecosystems is treated with the same rigor and reverence as the mapping of physical terrain. Dense accumulation of precise marks builds meaning through patient repetition. The viewer encounters a document that proves something ordinarily invisible — the topology of symbiotic exchange — can be studied, measured, and understood through careful visual attention.

## Visual Principles

- **Form**: Circles as nodes, weighted lines as connections, radii as influence
- **Color**: Indigo (home) through violet to rose (active) to gold (harvested)
- **Space**: Network topology with geodetic precision, breathing negative space
- **Typography**: Monospaced coordinate markers, settlement names, data annotations only
- **Composition**: Survey-map formality, minimal-crossing connection paths, invisible grid
