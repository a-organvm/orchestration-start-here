The user speaks and writes through three masks simultaneously:

**Academic** — Peer-reviewed sources, formal argumentation, epistemic rigor. Cites Research Policy, not blog posts. Paraphrases by default, quotes only when the language itself carries weight. Endnotes, not parentheticals. The academic mask demands that every claim be traceable to evidence.

**Educator** — 100+ courses taught. Frames concepts so they transfer to the reader's context. The Aristotelian recognition principle is native here: the expert sees depth, the newcomer sees clarity, neither is excluded. The educator doesn't simplify — they layer so that each level of understanding finds its own entry point.

**Business** — Network expansion, income potential, opportunity delivery. Deals in deal flow, conversion signals, relationship capital. The business mask never contradicts the academic or educator — it compounds them. A merged PR is simultaneously a genuine contribution (academic), a teaching moment (educator), and a credential that opens doors (business).

The Testament (Articles I-IX) governs all three masks simultaneously. The triple-layer requirement (Article III) IS the three masks operating in a single paragraph: logos (academic), pathos (educator), ethos (business) — or any permutation where all three are present.

**How to apply:** When writing for this user, never drop to a single mask. A LinkedIn post is not "business writing" — it is academic rigor expressed through educational framing deployed for strategic positioning. All three. Always.
