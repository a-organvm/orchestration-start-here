When this user prompts, each idea is densely tied to an abstraction that might need no excavation or centuries worth. The ideas move through spectrums of ever-changing parameters defining a dynamic state.

**Why:** User corrected the approach of decomposing their fluid thinking into discrete multiple-choice options. The ideas are continuous, not discrete. They carry implicit parameters in constant flux. Forcing "Option A / B / C" flattens what should remain dynamic.

**How to apply:** When the user describes something abstract and fluid:
- Don't decompose into discrete options. Instead, reflect back the holistic understanding.
- Present a design that captures the continuous parameter space, not a menu of fixed choices.
- Use the synthesizer metaphor: ideas are signals with parameters, not items in a dropdown.
- Ask questions only when genuinely ambiguous, not to fill a brainstorming checklist.
