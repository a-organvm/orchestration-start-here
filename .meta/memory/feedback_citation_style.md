Direct quotation only when the language itself is relevant and necessary to the point being made. Otherwise paraphrase to reframe as your own voice.

Every quotation, paraphrase, and citation needs the "quotation sandwich":
- Runway IN: introduce the source, give context for why it's being cited
- The citation: quote or paraphrase
- Runway OUT: land it, connect it back to the point being made

Use numbered endnotes (superscript in text, Notes section at end) — NOT inline parenthetical citations cluttering the prose.

**Why:** The user follows academic writing standards. Inline parentheticals break flow, especially in LinkedIn/essay contexts. Direct quotes should be rare and purposeful. The reader should never encounter a citation without understanding why it's there and what it means for the argument.

**How to apply:** Default to paraphrase. Reserve direct quotes for when exact phrasing carries rhetorical weight. Always sandwich. Always endnotes.
